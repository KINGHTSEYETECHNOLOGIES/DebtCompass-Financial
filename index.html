Understanding Your Options

What are the main debt relief options for unsecured debt?

If you are carrying $10,000 or more in unsecured debt — credit cards, personal loans, medical bills — there are several structured paths forward. The right option depends on your total debt amount, your credit score, your monthly income, and your goals. Understanding each option before speaking with a program is the best way to make a confident, informed decision.

What to Know Before You Enroll

What to look for in an accredited financial relief program

Not all programs are equal. Before enrolling in any financial relief program, look for the following characteristics in any provider you consider.

  • No upfront fees — legitimate programs are prohibited from charging fees before delivering results
  • Accreditation with the American Fair Credit Council (AFCC) or American Association for Debt Resolution (AADR)
  • A+ or A rating with the Better Business Bureau and positive independent consumer reviews
  • A clear explanation of how the program may affect your credit score before you enroll
  • A transparent fee structure disclosed before you sign any agreement
  • State licensing and availability verified for your location
  • A free consultation with no obligation to enroll

Important: Debt relief programs involve real trade-offs. These programs may affect your credit score during the enrollment period. Results vary based on individual circumstances, creditor participation, and program completion. Always consult the program terms and consider speaking with a financial advisor before making a decision.

Who qualifies for a financial relief program?

Most accredited financial relief programs require a minimum of $10,000 in unsecured debt to enroll. Qualifying debt typically includes credit cards, personal loans, medical bills, and department store cards. Secured debts such as mortgages and auto loans generally do not qualify.

Programs are available in most US states, though availability varies by provider and location. A free consultation with any program in our partner network will confirm whether you qualify and what your options are — at no cost and with no obligation to enroll.
